## The single most important finding

**"roadside assistance orlando" gets ~300–400 searches/month at keyword difficulty 0–6.** That is an almost-open goal. The non-branded organic spots are held by small towing/roadside outfits and a couple of directories (Yelp) — not by anyone with strong SEO. AAA and the government Road Ranger program rank, but they don't compete for the "I need someone to come help me now and I'll pay" intent the way a dedicated local page can.

RoadZone Plus already has a homepage and service pages targeting this. The job is to make them the best, most complete, most trusted-looking result for this term and its variants — and to do the same for each service.

> Note on the "320/mo" figure: it shows up across four near-identical phrasings ("roadside assistance orlando", "...orlando fl", "...orlando florida", "...in orlando fl"). Google treats these as essentially one search. Realistic combined demand for this core concept is **~300–400/mo**, not 1,280/mo. Don't let the repeated number inflate expectations.

## Trend alerts (this is the part you asked about — what's rising)

For a local roadside business, "trending" means three concrete things, all actionable:

### 1. EV roadside / mobile EV charging — genuinely rising, real new demand
The Orlando SERP for "EV roadside / dead EV battery" now has **multiple dedicated local players** (ChargeUp EV Mobile, Bee Charged EV, Orlando EV Mobile Charging) plus AAA, a Toyota of Orlando explainer, and 2025 buyer-guide content. This niche barely existed a couple of years ago. Two ways to play it:
   - **Deploy now, no new equipment:** EVs have a 12V auxiliary battery that dies exactly like a gas car's — and a dead 12V is the #1 reason an EV "won't start." RoadZone's existing **jumpstart service already helps most stranded EV owners.** Add honest content: "EV won't start in Orlando? It's usually the 12V battery — we can jump it." Captures EV searchers truthfully without claiming charging you can't do.
   - **Decision needed (bigger investment):** Actual mobile *charging* (delivering range to a depleted traction battery) requires equipment RoadZone likely doesn't have. This is a growth lane to evaluate, not an immediate page. ⚑ **Founder decision: do we want to enter mobile EV charging, or just own the 12V-EV-jumpstart angle?**

### 2. Tourist / rental-car roadside near the theme parks — underserved, Orlando-specific
The SERP for "locked keys in rental car orlando" surfaces rental companies (Hertz, Dollar, Avis, Alamo), the Disney Car Care Center, TripAdvisor/Facebook tourist threads — including a Disney-group post warning about an **overpriced locksmith with a 1hr 46min wait.** That's an unmet-need signal in plain sight. RoadZone already covers **Lake Buena Vista, Celebration, and Kissimmee** — the resort corridor. Content angles: "roadside help near Disney World / Universal," "out of gas near the parks," "locked keys in your rental car in Orlando." High intent, visitors who can't shop around, and almost no roadside company targets them directly.

### 3. Florida summer = peak roadside season, and it starts NOW
It's June 1. Florida heat is the single biggest seasonal driver of roadside calls: heat kills car batteries (jumpstart + battery replacement spike), hot asphalt causes blowouts (tire change spikes), and AC strain stresses charging systems. **Demand for clusters 3 and 4 climbs through Sept.** Publishing/optimizing the jumpstart, battery, and tire pages *this month* lines up with the seasonal wave instead of chasing it.

## Recommendations (in order)

1. **Own "roadside assistance orlando."** Optimize home + `/services` hub: tightest title/H1, clear 24/7 + service list + coverage, repeated click-to-call, FAQ schema (already present per the readiness doc). This is the #1 ROI move. (KD 0–6.)
2. **Reframe the fuel page around *emergency / out of gas*,** not generic "fuel delivery." Target "ran out of gas orlando," "emergency fuel delivery orlando," "out of gas near me." Avoids the crowded fleet/diesel field entirely.
3. **Publish a best-in-class wheel-lock-removal page + a companion explainer** ("lost your wheel lock key in Orlando? here's what to do"). Cheap to do, near-guaranteed to rank, differentiates the brand, and feeds tire-change jobs.
4. **Tighten the jumpstart, battery, and tire pages now for summer.** Add heat-related "signs" content; these clusters peak June–Sept.
5. **Add an EV no-start (12V) section to the jumpstart page** — honest, ranks against a rising trend, no new equipment. Hold the full mobile-EV-charging decision for you to make.
6. **Build a resort-corridor / tourist content piece** ("roadside help near Disney, Universal & the resort area") linking to the Lake Buena Vista, Celebration, and Kissimmee area pages.
7. **Verify each `/areas/[slug]` page targets "[service] [city]"** with real local detail (roads, landmarks), so the 13 area pages quietly accumulate long-tail traffic.
8. **Keep compliance tight** (per readiness doc): no pricing, arrival-time, rating, or certification claims unless provably true. The "free roadside assistance" page especially must be honest.

### Keywords to AVOID
- Branded competitor/AAA terms ("aaa orlando roadside assistance," KD 43) — you can't and shouldn't rank for these.
- Generic national "fuel delivery service" head term (KD 31, mostly fleet/B2B intent) — wrong audience, wrong intent.
- Full mobile-EV-charging terms **until** the equipment decision is made — don't promise a service you can't deliver.
